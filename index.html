Welcome to "Opening the Black Box", a blog dedicated to exploring and explaining how machines learn.

My name is Ryan Saxe, and I'm a Senior Machine Learning Research Scientist at PepsiCo. This blog is where I will share my ideas and understandings of current Machine Learning algorithms. Specifically, I hope to shed light on how the mathematical structures behind these algorithms inform how, and what, they learn.

Disclaimer: the content present here is my own and does not necessarily represent the views of my employer or colleagues.

  • Neural Networks as Interpretable as Linear Models

    The classic Neural Network (NN) with many layers and non-linear activation functions is difficult to interpret. However, that doesn't mean all NNs are opaque. This post delves into the history of General Linear Models (GLMs), and shows that with intentional design, NNs can be just as transparent.
